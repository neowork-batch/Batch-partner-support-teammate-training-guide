Before training begins, understand what you are here to do — and that this role will grow with Batch's needs.

The most common confusion — know this

Web Partner and Batch Direct both involve the partner's own website — but they are completely different. With Batch Direct, the customer goes to the partner's site themselves via a link. With Web Partner, the customer books on Batch, and YOU go to the partner's site and place the booking manually. Step 3 — marking it confirmed in Batch — is what closes the loop. Without it, the customer has no confirmation.

3

Without this, the customer has no confirmation. Always complete the loop.

Day 3 · Proactive Work: QA & Outreach
Listing QA and Partner Outreach

96% of partners who churned had incomplete or unoptimized listings. This is the most impactful proactive work you do.

The goal of today

"You optimize listings before partners ask — then you tell them what you improved. You are not waiting for problems. You are preventing them."

Full listing QA — 17 steps in order

Where to start: Vendor Directory → Recently Live → NeoWork tab → scroll to the bottom (oldest listings first). Basic and Core only. Mark Airtable complete only after ALL 17 steps are done.

1. Experience title

Title Case: connecting words lowercase. 40–70 characters. No "number one" — use "highly recommended" or "highly sought".

2. Batch city

Verify the city matches the partner's actual location. Confirm in the booking URL and description.

3. Booking URL

Must include utm_source=batch. Open and test it. Confirm correct city and correct experience. Critical for multi-location partners.

4. Duration

Verify against the partner's website. Must match exactly.

5. Starting price

Check for accuracy. Lower pricing on Batch than their site is acceptable.

6. Location

Must be the partner's location — never the customer's. Verify it matches the actual business address.

7. About this experience

Scan for typos, duplicates, placeholder text. Remove duplicate "What You'll Do" headers. Use CoStar for optimization in Step 16.

8. Media library

No external watermarks. Alternate video and photo — no consecutive videos. Best photo first. Watch every video fully — no skipping.

9. Attachments

Menus, maps, schedules only. Disclaimers go here — never in media. Empty is fine.

10. Ratings and reviews

Minimum 10 required. If fewer, ask the partner to send review links so you can add them.

11. FAQs

Copy from the partner's website. Skip FAQs that contradict the listing. One question, one answer per item.

12. Booking notes

Review if present. Empty is fine. Flag anything confusing.

13. Availability

Must be empty for Batch Direct. Only populate for Email and Web partners.

14. Admin tags

Add all relevant experience tags. Most partners have far too few. Use CoStar for suggestions.

15. Partner profile page

Logo, about, website, Facebook numeric ID (not @handle), Instagram, TikTok, public phone. All fields complete.

16. Optimize title and description via CoStar

Run the CoStar prompt. Remove AI markers (em-dashes, "furthermore"). Save changes. Refresh to confirm.

17. Send partner email + mark Airtable complete

Email: list what was improved and what opportunities remain. Mark complete only after all 16 steps above are done.

CoStar SEO tool

What it does

  • Generates keyword-rich title and description suggestions
  • Suggests relevant experience tags
  • Rewrites descriptions for SEO without losing the partner's voice
  • "Batchification" = running CoStar = optimizing for Batch's search algorithm

How to use it

  1. Copy the partner's existing description
  2. Paste into CoStar with the partner name and city
  3. Review — remove AI markers (em-dashes, "furthermore," "delve")
  4. Paste the improved version back into the listing and save
  5. Allow 20–25 minutes per full optimization

1

Day 5 · Retention Foundations
The SAVE Framework and 4 Retention Levers

You will use this framework on every single churn call you ever take. Learn it until it feels natural — not scripted.

SAVE framework — every churn call, every time
SAVE framework at a glance
S
Sympathize — acknowledge frustration first, before anything else

"I completely understand — I am really glad you reached out. Let me pull up your account right now."

A
Assess — pull their dashboard and read the numbers aloud

"In the last 30 days you have had X page views, X favorites, and X itinerary adds."

V
Value — translate every number into plain language

Never say "127 page views." Say "127 potential customers saw your business last month." Use the SEO 3-month argument if they are in Month 1–3.

E
Expand — offer Tier 1 first (always free)

"Here is what I would like to do for you at no extra charge before you make any decision."

D
Decide — guide toward a mutual outcome

Stay (with or without an offer) or cancel gracefully. If the partner is fully decided — stop offering and proceed to cancellation cleanly.

Four retention levers — always in this order
Critical rule

Never jump to Tier 3 without exhausting Tiers 1 and 2. Skipping ahead devalues the offer and teaches partners to push harder for bigger discounts.

1
Full optimization package — free, always first

Listing optimization, blog post, UGC tool, ratings, media shuffle, profile. 96% of churned partners had incomplete listings.

2
Switch quarterly to monthly billing

No price change. Reduces the commitment burden. Offer when the concern is commitment size, not cost.

3
Temporary price reduction — max 3 months

25% off for 2 months → $150/month → $99/month. Each step only if the previous was refused. Always set via Stripe phasing — never a direct price change.

4
Bookable partner conversion — final offer

Convert Batch Direct to bookable so the partner sees actual booking ROI inside Batch. Can combine with $99/month.

When to save vs when to let go

Worth saving — use every lever

  • Some traction: favorites, itinerary adds, or referral clicks in the data
  • Partner is in Month 1–3 (before SEO compounds)
  • Concern is about cost — price lever still available
  • No optimization has been done on their listing yet

Let them go gracefully

  • Zero itinerary adds after 90+ days of activity
  • Partner already received a prior discount
  • Partner is fully decided — repeating the same objection
  • Prior bad experience that cannot be undone
What to say

"I completely respect your decision. Let me process the cancellation to the end of your billing period. You keep full access until then. Thank you for being a Batch partner."

Day 7 · Disputes & Past Due Accounts
Handling High-Stakes Situations

Disputes are won on documentation quality. Build your evidence file the same day a dispute comes in — never wait until the deadline.

Dispute management
The 7-day rule

Stripe gives you 7 days to respond to a dispute. Enter every new dispute into Airtable the same day you find it. Build the evidence package same day. Missing this deadline means you automatically lose.

Evidence package — build same day

  • Stripe payment receipts and full payment history (screenshots)
  • Intercom conversation screenshots — the full thread
  • HubSpot call logs and written notes
  • Optimization work proof — before and after screenshots of listing changes
  • Written cancellation confirmation if applicable

Dispute thresholds

  • Under $500: build evidence and submit counter-evidence independently
  • Over $500: build evidence, share with Stefan first, submit after his input

Prevention: always send written cancellation confirmation immediately after every churn call. This is your #1 dispute prevention tool.

Past due partner call process
1
Check the partner's time zone before calling

Look up the area code on Google. No calls after 6 PM their local time — ever.

2
Call once — leave voicemail if no answer

"Hi, this is [Your Name] from the partner support team at Batch. I am reaching out about your account — there is an outstanding invoice I would love to help you sort out. Please call us back or reply to our email. Thank you."

3
Document the attempt in Airtable comments — same day

Date, what was done, current status. Log every attempt.

4
After 3 attempts with no response — escalate

Three attempts = due diligence done. Proceed to cancellation with a final notification email.

Reporting accuracy — only log what is truly done

Log as complete only when fully done

  • "Optimization complete" — only after changes saved

Never log these as done

  • "Optimization in progress" — started is not complete
  • Any number you cannot point to a specific record to prove
